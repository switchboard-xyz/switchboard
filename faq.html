FAQ

Below are some frequently asked questions to help get you started with Switchboard.

Core‚Äã

What is Switchboard?‚Äã

Switchboard is a multi-chain, permissionless oracle protocol for general purpose data feeds and verifiable randomness (VRF).

Switchboard gets its name from the early days of phone networks, where operators would shuffle telephone lines around a 'switchboard' to complete a circuit and forward a dialer‚Äôs call to the next hop in the circuit. Similarly, Switchboard, our oracle network is responsible for how external data gets updated and routed through a blockchain until it reaches its appropriate destination.

Why do blockchains require an oracle?‚Äã

Blockchains are unable to acces off-chain information to themselves, hence oracle solutions are required to bridge this gap to enable on-chain applications to query information from the real world.

What is an oracle?‚Äã

Blockchains do not have the capability of directly relaying information from off-chain to on-chain. Hence, enter oracles, a gateway between blockchain and real world data.

An oracle is not the data source, it is an infrastructure layer for querying, verifying and authenticating external data sources; which is relayed to the blockchain for a smart contract to read and carry out necessary course of action for its intended product.

What is the 'oracle problem'?‚Äã

The oracle problem in blockchain is caused by the inability of blockchains to interact with external systems. Blockchains are isolated networks that only rely on data stored in their ledger to form a consensus on binary questions such as whether a public key holder signed a transaction with their corresponding private key, whether a public address has enough funds to cover its transaction, and whether a transaction type is valid within a smart contract. The deterministic nature of smart contracts is due to the narrow focus of blockchain consensus and their ability to execute exactly as written with a higher degree of certainty than traditional systems.

Incorrect reporting can lead to devastating outcomes for on-chain applications dependent on the data feeds. Different oracle solutions vary in their approach to solving the problem, often making different tradeoffs between relying on highly centralized, trusted data providers, or more-decentralized networks with economic and cryptographic security measures. Similarly, different use cases vary in their requirements for speed, fidelity, and resolution of their data feeds.

Other oracle solutions require developers to choose from a limited selection of data feeds, and offer no easy way to publish their own custom feed for the data sources they need. In a dynamic, changing world, this versatility is critical to unlock the full potential of decentralized applications.

How does Switchboard address the 'oracle problem'?‚Äã

Switchboard addresses the oracle problem in the following ways:

Permissionless: Developers can build their own data feeds, and optionally spin up an application-specific free-standing oracle network for their feeds, called an oracle queue.

Customization: Different feeds and queues can be configured with a variety of parameters, allowing developers to decide for themselves how to optimally balance decentralization, security, and speed for their own unique use case.

Decentralization: Developers can decide between flexible governance options, such as DAO governance, multisigs, privileged admins, or immutable definitions.

How decentralised is the Switchboard Network?‚Äã

Switchboard's oracle architecture consists of multiple staked nodes, our network page https://switchboard.xyz/network displays the number of oracles live. To understand the architecture better, navigate to the Network docs to deep dive.

What is Switchboard‚Äôs economic model?‚Äã

For operating data feeds, user has to fund an upfront capital (dependant on how the feed is configured) that consists of transaction fees and oracle incentives to ensure oracles are incentivised to publish good data. For a deeper look: https://docs.switchboard.xyz/feeds/cost

For utilizing VRFs (currently only on Solana), user has to pay 0.002 SOL on every request of randomness, similar to data feeds, the fees consists of mainly transaction fees (math and compute costs) and oracle incentives.

How does Switchboard ensure high-quality/reliable data to be curated/published on-chain?‚Äã

Switchboard is a permissionless oracle protocol. Hence, all data feeds are different based on its use cases and types of data needed. It is a best practice to ensure data feed reliability before utilizing for all purposes.

For a deeper look a Best Practices: (https://docs.switchboard.xyz/feeds/best-practices)[https://docs.switchboard.xyz/feeds/best-practices]

Switchboard ecosystem‚Äã

Who is part of the Switchboard ecosystem?‚Äã

The roler players who belongs to the Switchboard ecosystem consists of Oracle (node) operators, Curators, Publishers and the feed consumers themselves.

Find out more about the role players in the Switchboard ecosystem here: https://switchboard.xyz/ecosystem

Who is a Oracle (node) operator?‚Äã

Oracle operators are the engine of the network, who maintain and run oracles that exist between the blockchain and Web 2.

Who is a Curator?‚Äã

Curators¬†are the explorers of the network and are responsible for populatingfeed templates for Publishers to browse and build their data feeds.

Who is a Publisher?‚Äã

Publishers are the builders of the network and are responsible for browsing the catalog and building, configuring, funding and publishing the data feeds.

Who is a feed consumer?‚Äã

Feed consumers are the end users of the network such as protocols or developers. Protocols can be utilizing them to plug into their smart contracts to power their specific applications; whereas developers can be utilizing them to start their own side projects or learning to put oracles to use.

They are encouraged to help fund the leases of the utilized-feeds.

Products‚Äã

What is Switchboard Explorer?‚Äã

Explorer is an explore page where users scour for published feeds (that has fixed parameters) and are able to take a feed‚Äôs public key to integrate it into their programs.

What is Switchboard Publisher?‚Äã

The Publisher is a Switchboard application that allows a feed creator to bring any data they need on-chain in under 60 seconds. Before the publisher, feed creators would need to sign partnership deals with existing oracle providers which resulted in a weeks long process.

The Publisher allows a user to:

  • Browse existing jobs in the catalog
  • Build a job from a set of pre-curated templates like fetching from common centralized exchanges
  • Use the Build-Your-Own-Feed tool to create custom jobs
  • Test new jobs
  • Create new data feeds
  • Manage and monitor their feeds

What is Switchboard Verifiable Random Function (VRF)?‚Äã

Blockchains are not hardware, hence, true randomness is not achievable. Switchboard‚Äôs VRF was modelled after Algorand‚Äôs pseudo-random function implementation, allowing verifiable randomness to be generated on-chain. Use cases such as Casinos, game theory, NFT minting mechanism can be relied on with VRF.
